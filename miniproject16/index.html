Mini Project Exhibition - 2016

MINI PROJECT EXHIBITION

IEEE MSRIT conducted a mini project exhibition on November 26, 2016 for first and second year students which gave an opportunity for them to exhibit their creativity and to bring innovative ideas of students to practice. The participants had to build projects using arduino as the main component and they were given the freedom to choose the projects, the exhibition was open for all the first and second year students.

For further guidance third year students were assigned to every team as mentors to help them with their projects. Each team had around 4-5 students. The exhibition was held at LHC and DES blocks and the event was inaugurated at 12:00p.m and it went on till 2:00p.m.

The judges for the event were Dr. S Sethu Selvi, professor and head of EC department and Manjunath sir, professor in EC department. The projects were judged based on the criteria’s like creativity, understanding, working etc. About 27 teams had participated in the competition and all the participants were very enthusiastic to exhibit their projects. They also explained the working of their projects and the concepts involved in it to the judges and the spectators.

There were projects like:

  • Automatic plant watering system
  • Designing robot
  • Home automation system
  • Remote controlled car
  • Mini prosthetic hand
  • Multipurpose surveillance robot
  • Arduino drawing machine
  • Smart line follower
  • Gesture controlled robot
  • Maze solver robot
  • Color sorting robot
  • Hand control car
  • Smart blind man stick
  • Increasing solar efficiency using automatic, waterless solar panel cleaner
  • Motion car
  • Persistence of vision
  • Automated water supply
  • Line follower robot with obstacle detector and remover
  • Light seeker and Bluetooth controlled land rover
  • Light following robot and many more


The event was helpful in making the students understand the practical difficulties encountered while developing a project. They understood the various concepts related to their projects.

The best projects were rewarded and the event was successful with a large number of spectators.
